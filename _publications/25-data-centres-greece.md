This article examines how the Greek state facilitated Microsoft's construction of three data centres in Attica under the post-pandemic Greece 2.0 recovery plan. Based on official communications, regulation, and legal documents, it shows how state-led modernisation policy can consolidate corporate infrastructural power while shifting environmental and social costs onto local communities.
